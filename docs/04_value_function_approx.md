# Value Function Approximations and Experimental Setup

**Source:** Löhndorf & Minner (2009), §4.

---

## 1. Polynomial Basis Functions

The Weierstrass approximation theorem guarantees that any continuous function on a closed interval can be approximated arbitrarily closely by a polynomial. The paper exploits this by using polynomial expansions of the state–action vector as basis functions for $\bar{V}(S, x; w)$.

Define the augmented state–action vector:

$$z = \{x, y, p, g\} \quad (z_1 = x,\; z_2 = y,\; z_3 = p,\; z_4 = g)$$

### Second-order polynomial (LSAPI-2)

$$\bar{V}_2(S, x) = w_0 + \sum_{i=1}^{4} w_i z_i + \sum_{i=1}^{4} \sum_{j=i}^{4} w_{ij} z_i z_j \tag{26}$$

Number of basis functions: $1 + 4 + 10 = 15$.

### Third-order polynomial (LSAPI-3)

$$\bar{V}_3(S, x) = w_0 + \sum_{i=1}^{4} w_i z_i + \sum_{i=1}^{4} \sum_{j=i}^{4} w_{ij} z_i z_j + \sum_{i=1}^{4} \sum_{j=i}^{4} \sum_{k=j}^{4} w_{ijk} z_i z_j z_k \tag{27}$$

Number of basis functions: $1 + 4 + 10 + 20 = 35$.

Note that $\bar{V}_2(S, x)$ is a subspace of $\bar{V}_3(S, x)$, so LSAPI-3 is at least as expressive as LSAPI-2 for sufficiently large sample sizes.

## 4. Solution Quality (Table 2)

The paper benchmarks LSAPI-2 and LSAPI-3 against the LP optimal discrete policy over 90 parameter configurations sampled from the Faure low-discrepancy sequence.

| Policy | Algorithm | Mean Reward |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Optimal Discrete Policy | LP | 19.94 |
| Second-order Polynomial | LSAPI-2 | 20.63 |
| Third-order Polynomial | LSAPI-3 | 20.74 |

Both ADP policies achieve higher mean rewards than the LP optimal discrete policy (~3.5% and ~4.0% improvement respectively, $p < 0.01$). The difference between LSAPI-2 and LSAPI-3 is not statistically significant.

The ADP advantage arises because the continuous-state polynomial policy avoids the rounding errors introduced by discretisation, especially when $\eta < 1$.

## 5. Metamodel — Sensitivity Analysis (§4.4)

To study the sensitivity of the discounted reward to model parameters, a regression metamodel is estimated over 2000 LSAPI-2 runs generated by the space-filling Faure design.

Denote $Z = (Z_1, \ldots, Z_{10})$ as the parameter vector (indexed as in Table 1 above).

**Main effects model:**

$$\hat{Q}_1 = \beta_0 + \beta_1 Z_1 + \beta_3 Z_3 + \beta_5 Z_5 + \beta_6 Z_6 + \beta_9 Z_9 + \beta_{10} Z_{10} \tag{28}$$

($R^2 = 0.53$; captures direct impact of variability, capacity, correlation, and imbalance costs.)

**Interaction effects model** (adds storage-capacity interactions and autocorrelation interactions):

$$\hat{Q}_2 = \hat{Q}_1 + \beta_{12} Z_1 Z_2 + \beta_{345} Z_3 Z_4 Z_5 + \sum_{i=1}^{10} \beta_{i6} Z_i Z_6 \tag{29}$$

($R^2 = 0.898$; substantially better fit.)

### Key findings from $\hat{Q}_1$ (Table 4)

| Parameter | Effect | Interpretation |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| $\sigma_Y$ ($Z_1$) | $\beta_1 < 0$ | Higher supply variability → higher imbalance costs |
| $\sigma_P$ ($Z_3$) | $\beta_3 < 0$ | Higher price variability → higher imbalance costs (smaller effect than $\sigma_Y$ due to unilateral dependence) |
| $\rho_{PY}$ ($Z_5$) | $\beta_5 > 0$ | Positive price–supply correlation increases rewards (beneficial for solar) |
| $C$ ($Z_6$) | $\beta_6 > 0$ | More storage capacity → higher rewards |
| $u$ ($Z_9$) | $\beta_9 < 0$ | Higher negative imbalance penalty → lower rewards |
| $o$ ($Z_{10}$) | $\beta_{10} > 0$ | Higher positive imbalance payout → higher rewards |

### Key findings from $\hat{Q}_2$ interactions (Table 5)

| Interaction | Effect | Interpretation |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| $Z_1 Z_2$ ($\sigma_Y \times \theta_Y$) | $\beta_{12} > 0$ | Autocorrelation dampens the negative impact of supply variability |
| $Z_3 Z_4 Z_5$ ($\sigma_P \times \theta_P \times \rho_{PY}$) | $\beta_{345} > 0$ | Three-way interaction: autocorrelation also dampens price-variability impact |
| $Z_2 Z_6$ ($\theta_Y \times C$) | $\beta_{26} < 0$ | Autocorrelation decreases value of storage (wider amplitude needs more buffer) |
| $Z_4 Z_6$ ($\theta_P \times C$) | $\beta_{46} < 0$ | Same effect for price autocorrelation |
| $Z_6 Z_6$ ($C^2$) | $\beta_{66} < 0$ | Diminishing returns to storage capacity (concave storage value) |
| $Z_7 Z_6$ ($\eta \times C$) | $\beta_{76} > 0$ | Storage efficiency amplifies value of capacity |
| $Z_5 Z_6$ ($\rho_{PY} \times C$) | $\beta_{56} < 0$ | Negative correlation of price and supply increases storage value (arbitrage) |
